Attribution Method
==================

Blur Integrated Gradients (BlurIG)
-----------------------------------
Xu, S., Venugopalan, S., & Sundararajan, M. (2020).
Attribution in Scale and Space.
In Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and
Pattern Recognition (CVPR), pp. 9744-9753.

URL: https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_CVPR_2020/papers/Xu_Attribution_in_Scale_and_Space_CVPR_2020_paper.pdf

What it does:
  Standard Integrated Gradients requires a user-chosen baseline (e.g. a
  black image). That baseline is out-of-distribution for models like CLIP
  that have never seen a pure-black input, introducing artifacts in the
  integration path.

  BlurIG replaces the straight-line path from baseline to input with a
  progressive-deblur path:

      x(t) = GaussianBlur(x, sigma(t))
      sigma(t) = sigma_max * (1 - t),  t in [0, 1]

  At t=0 the "baseline" is the maximally blurred image (near-uniform,
  in-distribution). At t=1 it is the original input. Every interpolation
  step remains in natural-image space. The method satisfies the same
  axiomatic properties as standard IG (completeness, sensitivity,
  implementation invariance) while producing cleaner, less noisy maps.
